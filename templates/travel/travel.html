Travel Advice for Nepal

Stay Hydrated

Always carry a reusable water bottle to stay hydrated, especially during treks in Nepal's hilly and mountainous regions.

Respect Local Culture

Show respect for Nepalese culture by learning a few phrases in Nepali and following local customs, especially in temples and villages.

Be Weather Ready

Nepal's weather can change rapidly. Pack layers, a raincoat, and sunscreen to stay comfortable during your journey.

Plan Your Treks

Choose treks suitable for your fitness level and ensure you have the right permits for trekking in Nepal's protected areas.

Currency and Costs

Always carry some cash as ATMs may not be available in remote areas. The local currency is Nepalese Rupee (NPR).

Eco-Friendly Travel

Help preserve Nepal's beauty by avoiding plastic waste and following the "leave no trace" principles during your trip.
